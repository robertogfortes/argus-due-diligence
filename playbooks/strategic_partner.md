# Playbook: Strategic Partner Due Diligence

**Engagement Type:** `strategic_partner`
**Risk Exposure:** High (reputational co-mingling, financial exposure, IP sharing)
**Typical Investigation Depth:** Deep across all dimensions

---

## When to Use This Playbook

Use for joint ventures, co-development agreements, distribution partnerships, licensing deals,
or any relationship where your company's brand, IP, or strategic roadmap is linked to a third party.

---

## Investigation Dimensions & Depth

### 1. Financial (Deep)
Strategic partnerships often involve shared investment, profit-sharing, or revenue guarantees.
Financial health directly determines whether the partner can hold up their end long-term.

- Full financial health assessment using available public signals
- Ownership structure and UBO mapping (critical: who ultimately controls this partner?)
- Recent capital raises, investor base, and burn rate signals
- Any pending mergers, acquisitions, or changes of control
- Related-party transactions that could create conflicts of interest

**Key questions:**
- Is this partner financially strong enough to commit to a multi-year strategic relationship?
- Are there ownership changes pending that could affect the partnership?
- Is the investor base compatible with our company values and compliance requirements?

### 2. Legal & Compliance (Deep)
IP sharing and branding overlap make legal due diligence critical.

- Full litigation history (active + past 5 years)
- IP ownership verification (do they actually own what they claim?)
- Regulatory standing in all jurisdictions where partnership will operate
- Anti-corruption compliance track record (FCPA, LGPD, GDPR relevance)
- Contractual obligations with competitors that may create conflicts
- Director and executive background checks

**Key questions:**
- Are there IP disputes that could affect what they're bringing to the partnership?
- Is there regulatory exposure that could become our exposure through association?
- Are key executives or directors under investigation?

### 3. Reputational (Deep)
Strategic partnerships create reputational co-mingling — their reputation becomes ours.

- Comprehensive media coverage analysis (5-year lookback)
- ESG standing and controversies
- Social media sentiment and brand perception
- Industry associations and what peers say about them
- CEO/founder background and public profile
- Customer/client reference signals

**Key questions:**
- Would being publicly associated with this partner help or hurt our brand?
- Have they been involved in any ESG, ethics, or governance controversies?
- What do industry insiders say about them?

### 4. Operational (Deep)
- Technology stack and IP assets (if tech partnership)
- Actual customer base vs. claimed (reference checks recommended)
- Operational scalability for joint go-to-market
- Key person risk: is the business dependent on one or two individuals?
- Cultural and organizational alignment signals

**Key questions:**
- Do they have the operational capacity to grow with us?
- Are their claimed customers and references verifiable?
- What happens if their CEO leaves?

---

## Risk Thresholds for Strategic Partners

| Finding | Action |
|---|---|
| Active sanctions (any dimension) | DECLINE — mandatory |
| Undisclosed ownership or offshore shell controlling >25% | DECLINE or require UBO disclosure agreement |
| Major reputational crisis in past 24 months | Escalate to board before proceeding |
| IP disputes over assets relevant to partnership | DECLINE or require legal carve-out |
| Key person concentration risk | PROCEED WITH CONDITIONS + succession clause |
| Financial score < 50 | Require financial guarantees or escrow |
